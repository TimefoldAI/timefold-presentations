Why do LLMs fail here?

Scheduling is not a sequence of decisions

30 employees

150 shift assignments

1 shift: 30 options

2 shifts: 30*30 options

3 shifts: 30^3 options

150 shifts: 30^150 options

150 shifts: 30^150

3.69 √ó 10^221

10^80: atoms in the observable universe

10^221: 141 more zeroes

You can't optimize for such a search space by

predicting the next token
nor by reasoning like a human.

Surprised it works as well as it does, but there are still limits.
