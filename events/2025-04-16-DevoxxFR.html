Why do LLMs fail here?

Challenge:
Problem Size

The set of all possible solutions crafted from a given input

We have 14 employees , each with their own skills, availability, preferences, etc.

We schedule 7 days, there are 3 shifts per day and we need on average 3 employees per shift, potentially with different skills.

How many different solution can we create?

10^126 combinations (14 days, 3 shifts per day, on average 3 employees per shift)

14 * 3 * 3 = 126

10 employees => 10^126

10^80: atoms in the observable universe

10^126: 46 more zeroes

10^126 combinations (14 days, 3 shifts per day, on average 3 employees per shift) Now a lot of those solutions will be junk, but still!

You can't optimize for such a search space by
"predicting the next token"

Surprised it works as well as it does, but there are still limits.
